Welcome to the Instill AI x Hacktoberfest 2023! In this blog post, we will guide you on participating in this exciting event and making the most of your contributions. Whether you're a seasoned developer or a beginner looking to get involved, we have something for everyone. So let's dive in and discover how you can join the Hacktoberfest fun!

## Must-Read Information

Before you start contributing, there are a few important things to note:

### Hacktoberfest Registration

If you want to receive a Digital Reward Kit from Hacktoberfest or have a tree planted in your name through Tree Nation, make sure to register for a [Hacktoberfest](https://hacktoberfest.com/) account between September 26th and October 31st.

### Eligibility for Unique Digital Reward

Participants with four pull/merge requests accepted between October 1st and October 31st will receive a unique digital reward from Hacktoberfest. Please note that Instill AI also has its own rewards, so you have double the chance to earn recognition.

### Tree Planting in Your Name

The first 50,000 participants to have their first PR/MR accepted will have a tree planted in their name through Tree Nation. By contributing to the Instill AI Hacktoberfest, you can improve your programming skills and contribute to a greener planet!

### Contribution Period

Your PR/MRs must be created between October 1st and October 31st in any time zone (UTC-12 thru UTC+14). Make sure to make your PR/MRs to a public, unarchived repository.

### Acceptance Criteria

Instill AI must accept your pull/merge requests for them to count toward your total. PR/MRs that are labeled with a label containing the word `spam` or `invalid` by maintainers will not be counted. Only those labeled `hacktoberfest-accepted` will be considered.

## Swags

### Win Digital Instill Stickers

By sharing/reposting our event on social media, you'll earn Instill Digital Stickers.

### Win Swag Package

Participants whose credits rank in the **TOP 30%** will receive an Instill AI swag package, which includes **Instill Physical Stickers**, and **Instill Bottle or Mug**.

There are three primary ways to contribute and accumulate credits:

We have specific guidelines for each. Let's take a closer look:

### Issue Contributing Hackathon

Join the Issue Contributing Hackathon by following these steps:

1. Choose an issue from our [Community](https://github.com/instill-ai/community/issues?q=is:issue+is:open+label:hacktoberfest,+label:%22good+first+issue%22,%22help+wanted%22+) repository that you want to work on.
2. Leave a comment on the issue to indicate your interest in working on it. Our team at Instill AI will assign it to you.
3. After PR is sent and accepted, make sure to submit the Google Form [here](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to claim your credits. (Please submit multiple times for each task you complete)

#### Guidelines for Issue Contributing Hackathon

When participating in the Issue Contributing Hackathon, please adhere to the following guidelines:

- Each issue will only be assigned to one person/team at a time.
- Work on one issue at a time to maintain focus and ensure quality.
- Address all suggestions or feedback provided by the Instill AI team.
- Commit regularly to show progress. If no activity is observed within one week, the issue may be reassigned to another contributor.
- Join our [Discord](https://discord.gg/sevxWsqpGh) to engage in discussions and seek assistance if needed.

Here are the meaning of issue or PR labels:

- `hacktoberfest`: Only issues tagged will be counted in this event.
- `good first issue`: Issue suitable for first-time contributors
- `help wanted`: Issue complexity suitable for external contributors
- `bonus credits`: Earn 50 extra credits if accepted
- `to be claimed`: Issue available to be claimed. Please note that the issue will only be assigned to one person at a time.
- `in progress`: The issue has been assigned to a contributor. Please note that if there is no progress after one week, the assignment will be revoked and changed to `to be claimed`
- `pr submitted`: Once the PR is submitted. The status would be changed to `pr submitted`.
- `done`: Once the PR has been accepted, the status will be changed to `done`
- `hacktoberfest accepted`: After your contribution changes to `done`, youâ€™ll have to submit the [Google form](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to confirm. After further confirmation, the status will be changed to `hacktoberfest accepted`
  (Hacktoberfest will track this label)
- `invalid`: After reviewing, the PR is not accepted.
- `spam`: After reviewing, the PR is considered spam.

#### Rewards:

Each PR accepted: 50 credits

Each hard PR accepted(with extra-bonus labeled issue): 100 credits

### Pipeline Sharing Hackathon

To participate in the Pipeline Sharing Hackathon, follow these steps:

1. Create an application using VDP (Virtual Data Pipeline).
2. Share your pipeline on social media to earn 30 credits. (20 credits extra if includes GIF/video)
3. Submit your sharing/tutorial using the Google form [here](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to ensure our team reviews your submission. (Please submit multiple times for each task you complete)

#### Guidelines for Pipeline Sharing Hackathon

- Include a brief introduction of your pipeline, especially the use cases of the pipeline.
- Include a screenshot of your pipeline will be strongly recommended.
- Don't forget to include the hashtags `#instillhackathon` and `#hacktoberfest2023` to help us track your content.

#### Rewards

Pipeline sharing: 30 credits

Include GIF/video in the sharing: Extra 20 credits

### Video / Blog Tutorials Hackathon

To participate in the Video/Blog Tutorials Hackathon, follow these steps:

1. Complete Pipeline Sharing Hackathon
2. Create an end-to-end tutorial (video or blog) demonstrating how to use VDP (Virtual Data Pipeline) to create an application.
3. Share your tutorial on the platform of your choice and provide a brief description.
4. Submit your sharing/tutorial using the Google form [here](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to ensure our team reviews your submission. Remember, the tutorial must be in English and all content must be original. (Please submit multiple times for each task you complete)

#### Guidelines for Creating Video Tutorials

If you choose to create a video tutorial, follow these guidelines:

- Title: "How to Create an X Application Using VDP from Instill AI" (X can be anything)
- Discuss either the technical aspect or the use case aspect of the application.
- Include a voice-over to provide clear instructions.
- Ensure the video is of high quality and not pixelated.
- Publish the video on a public channel accessible to all audiences. Don't forget to include the hashtags `#instillhackathon` and `#hacktoberfest2023` to help us track your content.

#### Guidelines for Creating Blog Tutorials

For those who prefer writing, here are the guidelines for creating blog tutorials:

- Title: "How to Create an X Application Using VDP from Instill AI" (X can be anything)
- Discuss either the technical aspect or the use case aspect of the application.
- Include a hero image to make your tutorial visually appealing.
- Provide an introduction, step-by-step guide, and conclusion to guide readers through the process of building an application using VDP.
- Publish the tutorial on a public platform accessible to all audiences. If possible, include the hashtags `#instillhackathon` and `#hacktoberfest2023` to increase visibility.

#### Rewards

Each video/blog tutorial: 50 credits

## Hackathon Best Practices

To maximize your experience in the Instill AI x Hacktoberfest, we recommend following these best practices:

- Evaluate the complexity of the problem you intend to address and plan your approach accordingly.
- Clearly explain why and how you utilized Instill AI VDP in your video or blog tutorial.
- Provide a comprehensive explanation of your project to ensure clarity for readers or viewers.
- Collaborate with others to expedite the development process and enhance the final outcome.
- Don't hesitate to seek assistance or share your thoughts on our [Discord](https://discord.gg/sevxWsqpGh).
- Thoroughly test your work before submitting it to ensure its functionality and reliability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

If you ever have any questions, don't worry, we've got you covered right here!

### When is the hackathon taking place?

- Oct 1, 2023 - Hackathon Begins
- Oct 31, 2023 - Deadline for PR Submissions
- Nov 10, 2023 - Announcement of Top 30% Contributors

### What if I am interested in working on many issues?

Feel free to dedicate as many issues as you'd like. However, please remember that a contributor or team can only work on ONE issue at a time.

### What if I submit multiple video/blog tutorials?

You are welcome to submit multiple videos or tutorials, and the credits will add up!

### Can two or more people collaborate on the same project?

Absolutely! Collaboration is encouraged. However, only one person can submit the final project. If your team wins, the prize will be awarded to the individual who submitted the project.

### How can I create a video tutorial?

We recommend using QuickTime Player on your Mac or [Screen Studio](https://www.screen.studio/) for screen recording due to its high-quality output. Avoid using Loom, as it may produce pixelated videos.

### How will the top 30% of contributors be notified?

- The top 30% of contributors will be contacted via email using the address provided when submitting their projects. Additionally, we will announce the top contributors in the Instill AI newsletter.
