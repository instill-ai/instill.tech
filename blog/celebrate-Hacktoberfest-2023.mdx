### Contributing with code

Join the Issue Contributing Hackathon by following these steps:

1. Choose an issue from our [Community](https://github.com/instill-ai/community/issues?q=is:issue+is:open+label:hacktoberfest,+label:%22good+first+issue%22,%22help+wanted%22+) repository that you want to work on.
2. Leave a comment on the issue to indicate your interest in working on it. Our team at Instill AI will assign it to you.
3. After PR is sent and accepted, make sure to submit the Google Form [here](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to claim your credits. (Please submit multiple times for each task you complete)

When participating in the Issue Contributing Hackathon, please adhere to the following guidelines:

- Each issue will only be assigned to one person/team at a time.
- Work on one issue at a time to maintain focus and ensure quality.
- Address all suggestions or feedback provided by the Instill AI team.
- Commit regularly to show progress. If no activity is observed within one week, the issue may be reassigned to another contributor.
- Join our [Discord](https://discord.gg/sevxWsqpGh) to engage in discussions and seek assistance if needed.

Here are the meaning of issue or PR labels:

- `hacktoberfest`: Only issues tagged will be counted in this event.
- `good first issue`: Issue suitable for first-time contributors
- `help wanted`: Issue complexity suitable for external contributors
- `bonus credits`: Earn 50 extra credits if accepted
- `to be claimed`: Issue available to be claimed. Please note that the issue will only be assigned to one person at a time.
- `in progress`: The issue has been assigned to a contributor. Please note that if there is no progress after one week, the assignment will be revoked and changed to `to be claimed`
- `pr submitted`: Once the PR is submitted. The status would be changed to `pr submitted`.
- `done`: Once the PR has been accepted, the status will be changed to `done`
- `hacktoberfest accepted`: After your contribution changes to `done`, youâ€™ll have to submit the [Google form](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to confirm. After further confirmation, the status will be changed to `hacktoberfest accepted`
  (Hacktoberfest will track this label)
- `invalid`: After reviewing, the PR is not accepted.
- `spam`: After reviewing, the PR is considered spam.

### Sharing your pipelines and create tutorials

To participate in the Pipeline Sharing Hackathon, follow these steps:

1.  Create a VDP pipeline on [Instill Cloud](https://console.instill.tech/?utm_source=blog&utm_medium=link&utm_campaign=celebrate-hacktoberfest-2023) by following the provided [guideline](https://github.com/instill-ai/community/blob/main/hacktoberfest2023/guide-to-sharing-your-pipelines.md)
2.  Share your pipelines on social media to earn 30 credits (or an additional 20 credits if it includes a GIF or video).
3.  Produce and share a comprehensive tutorial on your preferred platform, demonstrating your pipeline creation process. This tutorial can be in video or blog format, and you will earn 50 credits for it.
4.  Submit your sharing/tutorial using the Google form [here](https://forms.gle/2r91a7ChMLVnWLm37) to ensure our team reviews your submission. Please remember to submit multiple times for each completed task.

When you share your pipelines on social media, please

- Include a brief introduction of your pipeline, especially the use cases of the pipeline.
- Include a screenshot of your pipeline will be strongly recommended.
- Don't forget to include the hashtags `#instillhackathon` and `#hacktoberfest2023` to help us track your content.

If you opt to create a video or blog tutorial, please adhere to the following guidelines:

- **Title**: For instance, "How to Create an X Application Using VDP from Instill AI" (You can replace X with any relevant term).
- **Content**: Discuss either the technical aspect or the use case aspect of the application.
- **For a Video Tutorial**:
  - Incorporate a clear voice-over to provide comprehensive instructions.
  - Ensure that the video maintains a high quality and is free from pixelation.
- **For a Blog Tutorial**:
  - Include a captivating hero image to enhance the visual appeal of your tutorial.
  - Structure your content with an introduction, a step-by-step guide, and a conclusion to effectively guide readers through the process of building an application using VDP.
- **Publication**: Make sure to publish the video or blog tutorial on a publicly accessible channel. Don't forget to include the hashtags `#instillhackathon` and `#hacktoberfest2023` to assist us in tracking your content.

## Hackathon best practices

To maximize your experience in the Instill AI x Hacktoberfest, we recommend following these best practices:

- Evaluate the complexity of the problem you intend to address and plan your approach accordingly.
- Clearly explain why and how you utilized Instill AI VDP in your video or blog tutorial.
- Provide a comprehensive explanation of your project to ensure clarity for readers or viewers.
- Collaborate with others to expedite the development process and enhance the final outcome.
- Don't hesitate to seek assistance or share your thoughts on our [Discord](https://discord.gg/sevxWsqpGh).
- Thoroughly test your work before submitting it to ensure its functionality and reliability.

**Contribution Period**

Your PR/MRs must be created between October 1st and October 31st in any time zone (UTC-12 thru UTC+14). Make sure to make your PR/MRs to a public, unarchived repository.

**Acceptance Criteria**

Instill AI must accept your pull/merge requests for them to count toward your total. PR/MRs that are labeled with a label containing the word `spam` or `invalid` by maintainers will not be counted. Only those labeled `hacktoberfest-accepted` will be considered.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Don't worry, we've got you covered with answers to common questions!

### When are the Hacktoberfest 2023 events taking place?

- **October 1, 2023** - Hacktoberfest 2023 Kicks Off
- **October 31, 2023** - PR Submissions Deadline
- **November 10, 2023** - Announcement of Top 30% Contributors

### What if I want to work on multiple issues?

You're welcome to take on as many issues as you'd like. However, please keep in mind that a contributor or team can work on ONE issue at a time.

### What if I want to submit multiple video/blog tutorials?

Feel free to submit multiple videos or tutorials; your credits will accumulate!

### Can two or more people collaborate on the same project?

Absolutely! Collaboration is encouraged. However, only one person can submit the final project. If your team wins, the prize will be awarded to the individual who submitted the project.

### How can I create a video tutorial?

We recommend using QuickTime Player on your Mac or [Screen Studio](https://www.screen.studio/) for screen recording, as they produce high-quality output. Avoid using Loom, as it may result in pixelated videos.

### How will the top 30% of contributors be notified?

The top 30% of contributors will receive an email notification using the address provided when submitting their projects.
Additionally, we will announce the top contributors in the Instill AI newsletter.
